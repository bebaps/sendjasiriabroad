#SendJasiriAbroad The opportunity of a lifetime

Welcome and thank you for choosing to help #SendJasiriAbroad. You are here because you saw my video or were recommended by someone you know. Either way, I am glad you came.

The Global Leadership Program, in which I have been accepted, consists of 3 weeks in Paris, France, a day trip to Brussels, Belgium and a final week in Madrid, Spain between June 16 and July 11, 2014. This will mark the fourth year that Dr. Matt Cabot, Associate Professor at San Jose State University, has been able to organize a group of students to learn from some of the top Communications and Public Relations firms in the world.

As the program title suggests, we will study abroad to learn, first hand, what it means to be a Global Leader. Theoretically speaking, you may wonder what is the purpose of a Global Leader? Well, global leaders are those people who can see various differences that typically keep people apart and envision how those distinctions can be combined to create solutions to global problems; whether political, cultural, or social.

Every region on the globe is connected to a global market or soon will be, so it is safe to say that the entire human race and many of Earths other inhabitants are reliant on the conscious, sound, and mutually beneficial decisions of global leaders. Future generations will need inspiration to continue leadership in a positive and fair manner. Global leaders are people who can engage cross-culturally, discard their biases, and unite businesses, governments, and non-profits in order to achieve a common goal.

The Global Leadership Study Abroad Program utilizes Being Global: How to Think, Act and Lead in a Transformed World, a book written by √Ångel Cabrera and Gregory Unruh. Being Global teaches that global leaders are not born, but instead become who they are by cultivating a global mindset and attaining global entrepreneurship while gaining global citizenship.

#SendJasiriAbroad A little bit about Jasiri...

Developing a global mindset requires remaining curious, studying, and learning through others about the many places in the world. To become a global entrepreneur, the ability to create value is a necessity. And to earn global citizenship, the pursuance of opportunities that produce the maximum benefit for all parties involved and affected is crucial.

I began to tend to the ripening of my global mindset by researching and learning about the beautiful cultures I will have the opportunity to interact with. My global entrepreneurship has had a mental start with my creating ideas of how my travels can benefit the local businesses of both the city in which I live, Santa Cruz, and my hometown, Pasadena, California. My global citizenship, though, will be created here with you and I on this website.

We do not all have the obligation to become global leaders, but we will all have the choice to support the global leaders that can ethically lead the nations of the world. I am asking that you join me on my journey in cultivating the necessary qualities of the type of global leader that you would want in your corner.

Donating to send me abroad will help me to find those opportunities to create and spread value throughout the world by connecting programs with a social mission, entities wanting to truly serve the people, and businesses desiring to create innovative and ethical change.
